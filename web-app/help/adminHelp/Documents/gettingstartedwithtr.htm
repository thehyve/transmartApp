Getting Started with tranSMART

The tranSMART application reflects the efforts of Johnson & Johnson’s informatics group to integrate data from internal and external data sources within a single data warehouse, and to provide scientific end users the tools to search for, view, and analyze the data in the warehouse.

The core internal data is a historical base of immunology trials with associated biomarker data from gene expression, RBM, and SNP experiments, including both raw and analyzed data, as well as oncology datasets from internal collaborations. Additionally, tools are provided to search through Johnson & Johnson’s other data repositories, including Hydra, SharePoint, and some of Beerse’s data analysis pipeline.

External data sources include publicly available resources such as the Gene Expression Omnibus repository, Dana-Farber Cancer Institute GeneChip Oncology Database, and MeSH Ontology.

The tranSMART application presents scientists with a search tool to query this vast ocean of disparate data through a Google-like user interface.

As users become more sophisticated in developing patterns of search terms, they can save and share their efforts with fellow researchers. A second tool, called the Dataset Explorer, allows the properly authorized user to create and study cohorts of patients that have been involved in completed clinical research efforts. Dataset Explorer also provides the user with tools to compare an individual (or group) in one study against a person or cohort in another study.

There may be some minor differences between the UI objects illustrated in this guide and the ones you see on your screen.
