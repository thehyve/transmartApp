Defining Search Filters

You define search filters to retrieve just the studies, analyses, and analysis data that interest you.

This section describes how to use search keywords to define search filters. You can also select search filters from the Filter Browser (see Select Search Filters from the Filter Browser).

The search filters you define, including any selected from the filter browsers, are displayed in the Active Filters area (see Manage Active Filters).

Keyword Search

The following figure shows the controls for defining a keyword search:

To define a keyword search:

1.  Select one of the categories in the category dropdown control, or select All to search across all categories.

Categories are:

Category

Search Scope

All

All categories

Analysis

Types of analyses, such as Disease vs. Normal or Time Course.

Compound

Compounds administered during the study.

Data Source

The source of the data, such as JNJ Internal.

Data type

Analysis data types, such as gene expression or protein data.

Disease

Disease names, such as Asthma or Melanoma.

Experimental design

The nature of the study, such as in-vitro or Experimental Medicine.

Gene

Individual genes that you specify.

Genelist

Genes in the gene list that you specify.

Gene lists are defined through the tranSMART Gene Signature/Lists tab (see Gene Signatures and Gene Lists).

Genesig

Genes in the gene signature you specify.

Gene signatures are defined through the tranSMART Gene Signature/Lists tab (see Gene Signatures and Gene Lists).

Organism

The classification of the study subjects, such as Homo sapiens or Mus musculus.

Pathology

Classifications of pathologies, such as Stage I or Metastatic.

Pathway

Genes in the pathway that you specify.

Platform

The vendor of the testing platform, such as Affymetrix or Agilent.

Protein

Individual proteins that you specify.

Sample type

The type of sample tested, such as Skin, Blood, Colon.

Study

Study accession numbers, such as GSE2125 or E-CBIL-8.

Therapeutic areas

Therapeutic areas, such as Oncology or Immunology.

Treatment

Treatments administered during the study.

Trial

Johnson & Johnson clinical trial numbers.

 

2.  Specify part or all of a search keyword in the text field to the right of the category dropdown.

When you type at least one character in the field, tranSMART begins to search within the specified category, and lists keywords that contain the text you type anywhere within the keyword. Not case sensitive.

For example, when you select Experimental design from the dropdown and type VI into the keyword field, tranSMART lists the following keywords:

Up to 20 keywords can be displayed at one time. If you don’t see the one you want, type more characters into the field.

3.  Click the keyword you want, but do not press Enter or Return.

When you click the keyword, the following actions occur:

□    The search begins immediately, and a result is displayed in the right pane.

□    The associated search filter appears in the Active Filters area:

The keywords that tranSMART displays may not match any of the data in the data warehouse. If you click a keyword with no associated data in the data warehouse, a No results found message appears in Search Results.

You can add more filters by repeating the steps above, by selecting filters from the Filter Browser, or by any combination of these actions.
