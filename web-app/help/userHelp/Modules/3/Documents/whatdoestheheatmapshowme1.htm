What does the heat map show me? 

A heat map is a matrix of colored squares.  The color of each square represents the level of up- or down-regulation of an analyte in a given experiment, as follows:

■    Red:  Up-regulated.

■    Green or Blue:  Down-regulated.

■    Black:  Neither up-regulated or down-regulated.

■    Gray:  No relevant data exists for the analyte.

The brighter the color, the more strongly up-regulated (bright red) or down-regulated (bright green or blue) the analyte was in the experiment.
